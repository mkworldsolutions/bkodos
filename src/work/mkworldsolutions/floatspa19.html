Overview

Float Spa 19 is a spa that specializes in creating a relaxing environment using their float pods. The float pods help individuals float by using salt. Doing this allows clients to relax which lowers stress and helps with balancing the mind, body, and spirit.

I was tasked with fully designing their website to introduce their brand new spa to potential clients. This was the first big project for MK World Solutions and it was an interesting experience since we had never had experience with project managing before. It was a challenge gathering all the requirements involved, keeping the clients on topic, and gathering their content on time to complete the project.

Research

To receive the requirements for the website we met up with the owners of Float Spa 19 to discuss the needs and wants for their website and getting to know them since we had never met them before. They concluded that the goals for their website were:

  • To introduce themselves, floating, and other services they provide.
  • To share the location of where their spa is.

Research for Float Spa 19 was an enlightening one due to the fact that in the beginning, I had never heard of Floating before. I utilized Trello in the brainstorming phase of what I knew and didn’t know about floating and what was important for the clients to know before arriving.

Any information I gathered along the way be it website design research or information research I cataloged them within Trello which helped me quickly go back in forth while sketching or brainstorming ideas.

Sketching & Wireframes

For this step of the process, I utilized several websites I had saved during my research phase as a base of ideas for the website. I originally thought of doing a one-page site but during the process decided to change it to a multi-page site. This was due to my realizing there was too much information for a one-page site, also since it is a new site one of their main goals was to stand out for google searches and the like. I was advised by my developer that it is better to do multi pages for SEO purposes. For these reasons, I decided on a multi-page website.

The website is mainly information based on the goal of having the visitor book online, call or visit the spa. Because of this, I used more pictures, quick descriptions, and easy booking buttons.

After meeting up with the clients and getting an approval for the direction I was headed it was now time for the final UI.

Final UI & Hand Off

This step was one of the most challenging parts of this process, the reason being was gathering all the pieces from the clients. It was new for us to utilize a due date for content and because of this, the process was strained. There was a lot of back and forth from the clients having their copy and content and then confirming their copy and content they wanted to provide.

While the battle for the content raged on I worked on getting a color pallet, utilizing typography, and maintaining cohesiveness within the sections of the website.

While I was finalizing the UI I created a prototype to be able to share the finished project, I also like having prototypes while creating everything so I don’t forget any step or feature with clicking through the prototype. Also showing them to the client was helpful.

Conclusion

Through this process, I have learned a great deal about freelance and helping clients with their vision. The utilization of due dates for content is important, we learned due to having the designs on time rather than having repeating updates.

I greatly appreciate Float Spa 19 for allowing us to create their website it was fun, challenging, and insightful. Exactly what I expect from UX Design in general.

I also couldn’t have done this without my developer partner who helped me bring all of the designs to life and together helping Float Spa 19 succeed into the future.
